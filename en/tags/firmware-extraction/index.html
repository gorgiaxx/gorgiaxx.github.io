    Firmware Firmware, also called firmware image in some contexts (and sometimes referred to as “ROM” in mobile communities), resides in non-volatile memory (Non-Volatile Memory, NVM) and can be read and written. In embedded systems, the most common NVM types are ROM (read-only memory) and Flash memory. ROM includes Mask ROM, PROM, EPROM, and EEPROM. Today, the “mainstream ROM” is typically EEPROM, often integrated inside the MCU; meanwhile, the more common external storage is Flash memory. …
